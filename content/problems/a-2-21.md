## Given

An undirected graph $G = (V, E)$ with an ordering on the edges in $E$ and a designated edge $e \in E$.

## Problem

Is $e$ in the lexicographically first maximal $k$-cycle free edge induced subgraph of $G$? The lexicographically first maximal $k$-cycle free edge induced subgraph of $G$ is the graph formed by processing each edge in $E$ in order and adding it to the subgraph being built if it does not form a cycle of size $k$.

## Status

P-complete problem from Greenlaw et al. (1995), Problem A.2.21.

## Remarks

Miyano proves the problem is P-complete for several different fixed values of $k$. For $k = 3, 4, 5, 6$, or $k \geq 7$ with $G$ of maximum degree $6, 4, 4, 3$, or $3$ respectively, the problem is P-complete. For $G$ a planar graph and $k = 4, 5, 6$, or $k \geq 7$ with $G$ of maximum degree $5, 4, 4$, or $3$ respectively, the problem is P-complete. For $G$ a general graph and $k = 3$ or $4$ with $G$ of maximum degree $4$ or $3$ respectively, the problem is in $\text{NC}^2$ [264, 265]. See Problems A.2.17 and A.2.18 for results with a similar flavor. It is also interesting to contrast the results for the edge induced subgraph problem with those for the vertex induced subgraph problem; see Problem A.2.16. Also see Problem B.1.8 for related open questions. Lastly, note that for $k = 1$ this problem becomes Lexicographically First Maximal Matching, Problem B.8.2; it is in CC.
