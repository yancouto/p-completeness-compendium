## Given

An undirected graph $G = (V, E)$ with an ordering on the edges in $E$ and a designated edge $e \in E$.

## Problem

Is $e$ in the lexicographically first maximal $k$-cycle free edge induced subgraph of $G$? The *lexicographically first maximal $k$-cycle free edge induced subgraph of $G$* is the graph formed by processing each edge in $E$ in order and adding it to the subgraph being built if it does not form a cycle of size $k$.

## Status

$\P$-complete (Miyano [1, 2]).

## Remarks

Miyano proves the problem is $\P$-complete for several different fixed values of $k$. For $k = 3, 4, 5, 6$, or $k \geq 7$ with $G$ of maximum degree $6, 4, 4, 3$, or $3$ respectively, the problem is $\P$-complete. For $G$ a planar graph and $k = 4, 5, 6$, or $k \geq 7$ with $G$ of maximum degree $5, 4, 4$, or $3$ respectively, the problem is $\P$-complete. For $G$ a general graph and $k = 3$ or $4$ with $G$ of maximum degree $4$ or $3$ respectively, the problem is in $\NC^2$ [1][2]. See [MFVS]({{< relref "./a-2-17.md" >}}) and [EMAS]({{< relref "./a-2-18.md" >}}) for results with a similar flavor. It is also interesting to contrast the results for the edge induced subgraph problem with those for the vertex induced subgraph problem; see [LFMS(π)]({{< relref "./a-2-16.md" >}}). Also see [LF3Edge]({{< relref "./b-1-8.md" >}}) for related open questions. Lastly, note that for $k = 1$ this problem becomes [LFMM]({{< relref "./b-8-2.md" >}}); it is in $\CC$.
