## Given

Two undirected graphs $G = (V, E)$ and $H = (V', E')$. The vertices in $G$ and $H$ have maximum degree at most $k$, a constant independent of the sizes of $G$ and $H$.

## Problem

Are $G$ and $H$ isomorphic? $G = (V, E)$ and $H = (V', E')$ are isomorphic if and only if there are two bijections $f : V \to V'$ and $g : E \to E'$ such that for every edge $e = \\{u, v\\} \in E$, $\\{f(u), f(v)\\} = g(e) \in E'$.

## Status

Open problem from Greenlaw et al. (1995), Problem B.1.1.

## Remarks

Luks showed the problem is in $\text{P}$ [1]. Without the degree bound, the problem is in $\text{NP}$ but not known to be in $\text{P}$, nor is it known to be either $\text{P}$-hard or $\text{NP}$-complete. Lindell shows that the Tree Isomorphism Problem is in $\text{NC}$; see remarks for Problem B.9.9. Subtree Isomorphism is in $\text{RNC}$ (Gibbons et al. [2], Lingas and Karpinski [3]). See Problem B.9.9 for additional details.
