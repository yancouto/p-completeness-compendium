## Given

An encoding $\overline{\alpha}$ of a circuit $\alpha$ composed of comparator gates, plus inputs $x_1, \ldots, x_n$, and a designated output $y$. A comparator gate outputs the minimum of its two inputs on its first output wire and outputs the maximum of its two inputs on its second output wire. The gate is further restricted so that each output has fanout at most one.

## Problem

Is output $y$ of $\alpha$ true on input $x_1, \ldots, x_n$?

## Status

Open.

## Remarks

Cook shows that CCVP is $\NC$ equivalent to computing the lexicographically first maximal matching in a bipartite graph. Mayr and Subramanian show that this matching problem is $\NC$ equivalent to Stable Marriage, Problem B.8.3 <a href="#ref-1" class="reference-citation">[1]</a>. Sairam, Vitter, and Tamassia address the "incremental" complexity of CCVP in <a href="#ref-2" class="reference-citation">[2]</a>.
