## Given

An encoding $\overline{\alpha}$ of a Boolean circuit $\alpha$, inputs $x_1, \ldots, x_n$ and designated output $y$. In addition to the restrictions of [AM2CVP]({{< relref "problems/a-1-4.md" >}}), this version requires the circuit to be synchronous. 

## Problem

Is output $y$ of $\alpha$ true on inputs $x_1, \ldots, x_n$?

## Definitions

See [CVP]({{< relref "./a-1-1.md" >}}). A circuit is *synchronous* if each level in the circuit can receive its inputs only from gates on the preceding level.

## Status

$\P$-complete under $\NC^1$ reductions (Greenlaw, Hoover and Ruzzo [[1, Theorem 6.2.5]](#1)).

## Remarks

The reduction to show hardness is from [AM2CVP]({{< relref "./a-1-4.md" >}}).
