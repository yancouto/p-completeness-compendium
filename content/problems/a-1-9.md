## Given

An encoding $\alpha$ of a $(min, +)$ circuit $\alpha$ and rational inputs $x_1, \ldots, x_n$.

## Problem

Does $\alpha$ on input $x_1, \ldots, x_n$ output a nonzero value?

## Status

P-complete problem from Greenlaw et al. (1995), Problem A.1.9.

## Remarks

The above reduction works in any ordered semi-group with additive identity $0$ and an element $1$ such that $1 + 1 \geq 1 > 0$. If there is a nonzero element $1$ such that $1 + 1 = 0$ (e.g. in $\mathbb{Z}_2$), then reduce NANDCVP via $\neg(u \wedge v) \to 1 + \min(u, v)$. In a well-ordered semigroup where $0$ is the minimum element, one or the other of these cases holds. If the semigroup is infinite, the problem may not be in P.
