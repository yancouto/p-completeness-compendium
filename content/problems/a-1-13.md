## Given

A finite monoid $(M, \oplus, 1)$ containing a nonsolvable group, where $M$ is a finite set, $\oplus$ is an associative binary operation on the elements of $M$, and $1$ acts as an identity element; an encoding $\alpha$ of a circuit $\alpha$, whose gates are of type $\oplus$, inputs $x_1, \ldots, x_n \in M$, and designated output $y$.

## Problem

Does $\alpha$ evaluate to $y$ on input $x_1, \ldots, x_n$?

## Status

P-complete problem from Greenlaw et al. (1995), Problem A.1.13.

## Remarks

If $M$ is solvable the same problem is in DET, the class of problems NC1 Turing reducible to computing integer determinants, see page 248. Also see Problems A.1.8 and A.1.9.
