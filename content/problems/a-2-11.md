## Given

An undirected graph $G = (V, E)$ with a numbering on the vertices in $V$, and two designated vertices $u$ and $v$.

## Problem

Is there an elimination order on $V$: $v_1, v_2, \ldots, v_n$, satisfying the properties that $u$ is eliminated before $v$ and for $1 \leq i \leq n$, $v_i$ is the lowest numbered vertex of minimum degree in the $(i-1)$-st remaining subgraph of $G$?

## Status

$\P$-complete problem from Greenlaw et al. (1995), Problem A.2.11.

## Remarks

The problem defined in [365] is more restricted than the one presented here. Their graphs are also required to have the property that $u$ appears before $v$ in some minimum elimination sequence if and only if $u$ appears before $v$ in all minimum degree elimination sequences.
