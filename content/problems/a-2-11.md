## Given

An undirected graph $G = (V, E)$ with a numbering on the vertices in $V$, and two designated vertices $u$ and $v$.

## Problem

Is there an *elimination order* on $V$, $v_1, v_2, \ldots, v_n$, satisfying the properties that $u$ is eliminated before $v$ and for $1 \leq i \leq n$, $v_i$ is the lowest numbered vertex of minimum degree in the $(i-1)$-st remaining subgraph of $G$?

## Status

$\P$-complete (Vishwanathan and Sridhar <a href="#ref-1" class="reference-citation">[1]</a>, Greenlaw <a href="#ref-2" class="reference-citation">[2]</a>).

## Remarks

This is the complementary of [OHDVR]({{< relref "./a-2-10.md" >}}),

The problem defined in <a href="#ref-1" class="reference-citation">[1]</a> is more restricted than the one presented here. Their graphs are also required to have the property that $u$ appears before $v$ in some minimum elimination sequence if and only if $u$ appears before $v$ in all minimum degree elimination sequences.
