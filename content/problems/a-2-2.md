## Given

An undirected graph $G = (V, E)$ with an ordering on the vertices and a designated vertex $v$.

## Problem

Is vertex v in the lexicographically first maximal clique of G?

## Status

$\P$-complete (Cook [1]).

## Remarks

Finding a maximal clique is equivalent to finding a maximal independent set in the complement graph of $G$, see LFMIS. Stewart shows that *Ordered Greedy Clique* and *Ordered Remaining Clique* are both $\P$-complete [2]. These two desision problems are based on heuristic greedy algorithms for computing cliques.
