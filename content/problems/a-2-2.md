## Given

An undirected graph $G = (V, E)$ with an ordering on the vertices and a designated vertex $v$.

## Problem

Is vertex v in the lexicographically first maximal clique of G?

## Status

$\P$-complete (Cook <a href="#ref-1" class="reference-citation">[1]</a>).

## Remarks

Finding a maximal clique is equivalent to finding a maximal independent set in the complement graph of $G$, see [LFMIS]({{< relref "./a-2-1.md" >}}). Stewart shows that *Ordered Greedy Clique* and *Ordered Remaining Clique* are both $\P$-complete <a href="#ref-2" class="reference-citation">[2]</a>. These two desision problems are based on heuristic greedy algorithms for computing cliques.
