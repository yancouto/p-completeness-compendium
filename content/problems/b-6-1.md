## Given

A set of $n$ flat mirrors of lengths $l_1, \ldots, l_n$, their placements at rational points in the plane, a source point $S$, the trajectory of a single beam emitted from $S$, and a designated mirror $M$.

## Problem

Is $M$ hit by the beam emitted from $S$ within $n$ reflections? At the mirrors, the angle of incident of the beam equals the angle of reflection.

## Status

Open (Greenlaw, Hoover, and Ruzzo [2]).

## Remarks

The *General Ray Tracing Problem* is to determine if a mirror is ever hit by the beam. When the mirrors are points, that is have no length, the general problem is in $\NC$ [2]. In two or more dimensions, the general problem is in $\mathsf{PSPACE}$ (Reif, Tygar, and Yoshida [1]). In three dimensions, with mirrors placed at rational points, the general problem is $\mathsf{PSPACE}$-hard [1]. The general problem is open for all mirrors of a fixed size as well. See [1] for a detailed discussion.
