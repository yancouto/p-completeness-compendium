## Given

An undirected graph $G = (V, E)$ with an ordering on its edges and a distinguished edge $e \in E$.

## Problem

Is $e$ in the lexicographically first maximal matching of $G$? A matching is maximal if it cannot be extended.

## Status

Open problem from Greenlaw et al. (1995), Problem B.8.2.

## Remarks

LFMM is $\NC$ equivalent to Comparator Circuit Value, Problem B.8.1 [1]. This problem resembles Lexicographically First Maximal Independent Set, Problem A.2.1, that is $\P$-complete. A $\P$-completeness proof for LFMM would imply that Edge-weighted Matching, Problem B.1.3, is also $\P$-complete.
