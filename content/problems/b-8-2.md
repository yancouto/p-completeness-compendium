## Given

An undirected graph $G = (V, E)$ with an ordering on its edges and a distinguished edge $e \in E$.

## Problem

Is $e$ in the lexicographically first maximal matching of $G$? A matching is maximal if it cannot be extended.

## Status

Open.

## Remarks

LFMM is $\NC$ equivalent to Comparator Circuit Value, Problem B.8.1 <a href="#ref-1" class="reference-citation">[1]</a>. This problem resembles Lexicographically First Maximal Independent Set, Problem A.2.1, that is $\P$-complete. A $\P$-completeness proof for LFMM would imply that Edge-weighted Matching, Problem B.1.3, is also $\P$-complete.
