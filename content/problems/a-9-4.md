## Given

An integer $d$, a set $S$ of $n$ points in $\mathbb{Q}^d$, and a designated point $p \in \mathbb{Q}^d$.

## Problem

Is $p$ on the convex hull of $S$?

## Status

P-complete problem from Greenlaw et al. (1995), Problem A.9.4.

## Remarks

The result shows that Successive Convex Hulls, Problem A.9.5, for arbitrary dimension $d$ is P-complete. See Problem A.9.5. The convex hull of $n$ points in the Euclidean plane can be computed optimally on an EREW-PRAM in $O(\log n)$ time using $n$ processors (see Dessmark, Lingas, and Maheshwari [91]).
