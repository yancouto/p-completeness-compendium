## Given

A sublinear time bounded *cellular tree automaton* (CTA) $M$ and an input string $x$.

## Problem

Does $M$ accept $x$?

## Definitions

See Ibarra, Jiang, and Chang [1].

## Status

$\P$-hard (Ibarra, Jiang, and Chang [1]).

## Remarks

A *trellis automaton* can accept a $\P$-complete language (Ibarra and Kim [2]). This in turn can be simulated by a *linear iterative array* that in turn can be simulated by a cellular tree automaton. See [1] and [2] for definitions and further details.

A sublinear time one-way bounded CTA can also accept a $\P$-complete language [1]. In a *one-way* CTA, communication between vertices is bottom-up. Every language accepted by an oneway CTA can be accepted by a deterministic Turing machine in $(\log n)^2 / \log \log n$ space, where $n$ is the size of the input [1].
