## Given

Let $S$ be a finite set and $F$ be the free group generated by $S$. Let $\overline{S} = \\{s, s^{-1} \mid s \in S\\}$, where $s^{-1}$ denotes the inverse of $s$. Let $\overline{S}^\ast$ denote the set of all finite words over $\overline{S}$. Let $U = \\{u_1, \ldots, u_m\\}$, $V = \\{v_1, \ldots, v_p\\} \subseteq \overline{S}^\ast$, where $m = p \in \N$.

## Problem

Is there a $x \in \overline{S}^\ast$ such that $x^{-1} \langle U \rangle x = \langle V \rangle$?

## Status

$\P$-complete (Avenhaus and Madlener [[1]](#1)).

## Remarks

A polynomial time algorithm for the problem is given in [[1]](#1). The reduction to show hardness is from Equivalence of Right Cosets, see [IC]({{< relref "./ic.md" >}}).

The problem of determining whether $x^{-1} \langle U \rangle x$ is a subgroup of $\langle V \rangle$ is also $\P$-complete [[1]](#1).
