## Given

A directed graph $G = (V, E)$ with each edge $e$ labeled by an integer capacity $c(e) \geq 0$, two distinguished vertices, source $s$ and sink $t$, and a value $f$.

## Problem

Is there a *feasible flow* of value $f$, that is, is the value of the maximum flow into the sink greater than or equal to $f$?

## Status

$\P$-complete (Goldschlager, Shaw, and Staples <a href="#ref-1" class="reference-citation">[1]</a>, Lengauer and Wagner <a href="#ref-2" class="reference-citation">[2]</a>).

## Remarks

See Dinic <a href="#ref-3" class="reference-citation">[3]</a> or Ford and Fulkerson <a href="#ref-4" class="reference-citation">[4]</a> for basic definitions and polynomial time algorithms. The reduction from [AM2CVP]({{< relref "./a-1-4.md" >}}) produces exponential edge capacities in $G$. In a network with edge capacities expressed in unary, computing the magnitude of the maximum flow is in $\mathsf{RNC}^2$ (Feather <a href="#ref-5" class="reference-citation">[5]</a>) and a method for finding the flow in $\mathsf{RNC}$ is also known (Karp, Upfal, and Wigderson <a href="#ref-6" class="reference-citation">[6]</a>). If the network is restricted to being acyclic, MaxFlow remains $\P$-complete (Ramachandran <a href="#ref-7" class="reference-citation">[7]</a>). Flows in planar networks can be computed in $\NC$ (Johnson <a href="#ref-8" class="reference-citation">[8]</a>, Johnson and Venkatesan <a href="#ref-9" class="reference-citation">[9]</a>). *Two Commodity Flow* (2CF) is defined similar to MaxFlow except there are two sources and two sinks, and there are two separate flow functions for the commodities. Since MaxFlow is a special case of 2CF and 2CF is in $\P$ by results of Itai <a href="#ref-10" class="reference-citation">[10]</a> and Khachian <a href="#ref-11" class="reference-citation">[11]</a>, it follows that 2CF is $\P$-complete. Itai defined several other variants of 2CF that are also $\P$-complete. They are defined below. $(l, u)$-2CF is a restricted form of 2CF in which there are lower and upper bounds on the capacity of each edge. Selective $(l, u)$-2CF is defined to be a 2CF with lower and upper bounds on the sum of the two flows on each edge. Stein and Wein show that, although there is an $\mathsf{RNC}$ algorithm to approximate maximum flow, approximating the minimum cost maximum flow is $\P$-complete <a href="#ref-12" class="reference-citation">[12]</a>.
