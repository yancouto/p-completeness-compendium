## Given

Let $S$ be a finite set and $F$ be the free group generated by $S$. Let $\overline{S} = \\{s, s^{-1} \mid s \in S\\}$, where $s^{-1}$ denotes the inverse of $s$. Let $\overline{S}^*$ denote the set of all finite words over $\overline{S}$. Let $U = \\{u_1, \ldots, u_m\\} \subseteq \overline{S}^*$, where $m \in \mathbb{N}$ and let $x \in \overline{S}^*$.

## Problem

Is $x \in \langle U \rangle$? That is, is $x$ in the subgroup of $F$ generated by $U$?

## Status

$\P$-complete problem from Greenlaw et al. (1995), Problem A.8.6.

## Remarks

For a natural number $k$, $\text{GWPC}(k)$ and $\text{GWPC}(\leq k)$ are the generalized word problems for finitely-generated subgroups of countably-generated free groups where all words involved are of length exactly $k$ and at most $k$, respectively. Stewart shows $\text{GWPC}(k)$ and $\text{GWPC}(\leq k)$ are $\P$-complete for $k$ greater than two [1]. When $k$ equals two the problems are complete for symmetric logarithmic space ($\mathsf{SLOG}$). The Word Problem for a Free Group is to decide whether $x$ equals the empty word in $F$. This problem is solvable in deterministic log space (Lipton and Zalcstein [2]).
