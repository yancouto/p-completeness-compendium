## Given

An undirected graph $G = (V, E)$ in which each vertex in $V$ has an $O(|V|)$ fixed length, label sequence of $m$ distinct numbers from $\{1, \ldots, m\}$, a designated source vertex $s$, and a designated vertex $t$.

## Problem

Is vertex $t$ added to the Eden cluster formed on $G$? The initial cluster consists of vertex $s$. At each step $i$, $1 \leq i \leq m$, the vertex adjacent to the current cluster, whose $i$-th label is smallest, is added to the cluster.

## Status

P-complete problem from Greenlaw et al. (1995), Problem A.12.5.

## Remarks

Eden growth is a process that is used for studying tumor growth. See Machta and Greenlaw [249] for additional information about the parallel complexity of Eden growth and related growth models [142, 141].
