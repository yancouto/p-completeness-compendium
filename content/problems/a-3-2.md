## Given

An undirected graph $G = (V, E)$, a subset $U \subseteq V$, an integer $k$, and a designated vertex $v$.

## Problem

Is vertex $v$ on the $k$-th path found when constructing paths greedily from vertices in $U$? The paths are formed by taking lexicographically first paths from vertices in $U$. The set is maximal in the sense that no more paths joining vertices in $U$ can be added.

## Status

$\P$-complete (Anderson [1]).

## Remarks

The reduction to show hardness is from a restricted version of [CVP]({{< relref "./a-1-1.md" >}}).

The construction of maximal sets of vertex disjoint paths is important for developing fast, parallel, randomized algorithms for depth-first search (Aggarwal and Anderson [2], Aggarwal, Anderson, and Kao [3]). See [DFS]({{< relref "./b-9-2.md" >}}) and [MDP]({{< relref "./b-9-5.md" >}}).
