## Given

Let $S$ be a finite set and $F$ be the free group generated by $S$. Let $\overline{S} = \\{s, s^{-1} \mid s \in S\\}$, where $s^{-1}$ denotes the inverse of $s$. Let $\overline{S}^\ast$ denote the set of all finite words over $\overline{S}$. Let $x, y \in \overline{S}^\ast$. Let $U = \\{u_1, \ldots, u_m\\}$, $V = \\{v_1, \ldots, v_p\\} \subseteq \overline{S}^\ast$, where $m = p \in \N$.

## Problem

Is $\langle U \rangle x = y \langle V \rangle$?

## Status

$\P$-complete (Avenhaus and Madlener <a href="#ref-1" class="reference-citation">[1]</a>).

## Remarks

A polynomial time algorithm for the problem is given in <a href="#ref-1" class="reference-citation">[1]</a>. The reduction to show hardness is from [IS]({{< relref "./a-8-15.md" >}}).

The following three decision problems are also $\P$-complete <a href="#ref-1" class="reference-citation">[1]</a>:
1. *Equality of Right Cosets* --- Does $\langle U \rangle x = \langle V \rangle y$?,
2. *Equivalence of Cosets* --- Are there $x, y$ such that $\langle U \rangle x = y \langle V \rangle$?, and
3. *Equivalence of Right Cosets* --- Are there $x, y$ such that $\langle U \rangle x = \langle V \rangle y$?.
