## Given

Let $S$ be a finite set and $F$ be the free group generated by $S$. Let $\overline{S} = \\{s, s^{-1} \mid s \in S\\}$, where $s^{-1}$ denotes the inverse of $s$. Let $\overline{S}^*$ denote the set of all finite words over $\overline{S}$. Let $x, y \in \overline{S}^*$. Let $U = \\{u_1, \ldots, u_m\\}$, $V = \\{v_1, \ldots, v_p\\} \subseteq \overline{S}^*$, where $m = p \in \N$.

## Problem

Is $\langle U \rangle x = y \langle V \rangle$?

## Status

$\P$-complete (Avenhaus and Madlener [2]).

## Remarks

The following three decision problems are also $\P$-complete: Equality of Right Cosets — Does $\langle U \rangle x = \langle V \rangle y$?, Equivalence of Cosets — Are there $x, y$ such that $\langle U \rangle x = y \langle V \rangle$?, and Equivalence of Right Cosets — Are there $x, y$ such that $\langle U \rangle x = \langle V \rangle y$? [2].
