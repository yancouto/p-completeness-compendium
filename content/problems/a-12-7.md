## Given

A finite alphabet $\Sigma = \\{a_1, \ldots, a_n\\}$ with $n \geq 2$, two sets $P, N \subseteq \Sigma^\ast$ with $P \cap N = \varnothing$, and a positive integer $k$ less than or equal to $n$.

## Problem

Is the size of the alphabet $\Gamma$ obtained by the greedy alphabet reducing algorithm less than $k$?

## Definitions

The *greedy alphabet reducing algorithm* is given below:

<div>
\[
\begin{aligned}
&\text{begin} \\
&\quad \Gamma \leftarrow \Sigma \\
&\quad \psi(a) \leftarrow a \text{ for each } a \in \Sigma \\
&\quad \text{repeat} \\
&\qquad \text{for each } (a, b) \in \Gamma \times \Gamma \text{ with } a \neq b \\
&\qquad\qquad \text{let } \phi_{a\to b} \text{ be a mapping obtained by letting } \psi(\sigma) = b, \\
&\qquad\qquad\qquad \text{for all } \sigma \in \Sigma \text{ with } \psi(\sigma) = a \\
&\qquad\qquad \text{choose } \phi_{a\to b} \text{ satisfying } \tilde{\phi}_{a\to b}(P) \cap \tilde{\phi}_{a\to b}(N) = \varnothing \\
&\qquad\qquad\qquad \text{and minimizing } \left|\tilde{\phi}_{a\to b}(P) \cup \tilde{\phi}_{a\to b}(N)\right| \\
&\qquad\qquad \text{(Note: } \tilde{\phi}_{a\to b} \text{ denotes the natural homomorphism associated with } \phi_{a\to b}\text{.)} \\
&\qquad\qquad \text{remove } a \text{ from } \Gamma \\
&\quad \text{until no more replacements are found} \\
&\text{end.}
\end{aligned}
\]
</div>

## Status

$\P$-complete (Shimozono and Miyano [[1]](#1)).

## Remarks

The reduction is from [NANDCVP]({{< relref "./a-1-5.md" >}}).
Shimozono and Miyano also show that the general *Alphabet Indexing Problem* is $\NP$-complete, and the *Alphabet Indexing Local Search Problem* in the weighted case is $\mathsf{PLS}$-complete and in the unweighted case is $\P$-complete [[1]](#1).
