## Given

A finite alphabet $\Sigma = \\{a_1, \ldots, a_n\\}$ with $n$ greater than two, two sets $P, N \subseteq \Sigma^*$ with $P \cap N = \varnothing$, and a positive integer $k$ less than or equal to $n$.

## Problem

Is the size of the alphabet $\Gamma$ obtained by the greedy alphabet reducing algorithm less than $k$? The greedy alphabet reducing algorithm is given below. begin $\Gamma \leftarrow \Sigma$; $\psi(a) \leftarrow a$ for each $a \in \Sigma$; repeat for each $(a, b) \in \Gamma \times \Gamma$ with $a \neq b$ let $\phi_{a \to b}$ be a mapping obtained by letting $\psi(\sigma) = b$, for all $\sigma \in \Sigma$ with $\psi(\sigma) = a$; choose $\phi_{a \to b}$ satisfying $\tilde{\phi}_{a \to b}(P) \cap \tilde{\phi}_{a \to b}(N) = \varnothing$ and minimizing $|\tilde{\phi}_{a \to b}(P) \cup \tilde{\phi}_{a \to b}(N)|$; (Note, $\tilde{\phi}_{a \to b}$ denotes the natural homomorphism associated with $\phi_{a \to b}$.) remove $a$ from $\Gamma$; until no more replacements are found; end.

## Status

$\P$-complete (Shimozono and Miyano [3]).

## Remarks

Shimozono and Miyano also show that the general Alphabet Indexing Problem is NP-complete, and the Alphabet Indexing Local Search Problem in the weighted case is PLS-complete and in the unweighted case is $P$-complete [1][2][3].
