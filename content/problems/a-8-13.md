## Given

Let $S$ be a finite set and $F$ be the free group generated by $S$. Let $\overline{S} = \\{s, s^{-1} \mid s \in S\\}$, where $s^{-1}$ denotes the inverse of $s$. Let $\overline{S}^*$ denote the set of all finite words over $\overline{S}$. Let $U = \\{u_1, \ldots, u_m\\}$, $V = \\{v_1, \ldots, v_p\\} \subseteq \overline{S}^*$, where $m = p \in \mathbb{N}$.

## Problem

Does the mapping $\varphi$ defined by $\varphi(u_i) = v_i$ for $i = 1, \ldots, m$, induce an isomorphism from $\langle U \rangle$ to $\langle V \rangle$?

## Status

$\P$-complete (Avenhaus and Madlener [1]).
