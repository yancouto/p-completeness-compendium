## Given

An encoding $\alpha$ of a Boolean circuit $\alpha$, plus inputs $x_1, \ldots, x_n$, and a number $\epsilon \in (0, 1]$. Let $t$ denote the number of gates in $\alpha$ that evaluate to true.

## Problem

Find an integer $d$ such that $\lfloor \epsilon t \rfloor \leq d < t$.

## Status

P-complete problem from Greenlaw et al. (1995), Problem A.1.11.

## Remarks

The extension to $\alpha$ can be constructed to preserve properties such as monotonicity, fan-out, alternation, and planarity [327].
