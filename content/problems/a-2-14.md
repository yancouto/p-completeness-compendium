## Given

An undirected graph $G = (V, E)$ with a numbering on the vertices in $V$ and a designated vertex $u$.

## Problem

Is vertex $u$ in the dominating set formed by the execution of the greedy dominating set algorithm?

A *dominating set* $V'$ is a subset of $V$ such that for all $v \in V - V'$ there exists a vertex $w \in V'$ such that $\\{v, w\\} \in E$. We say $w$ covers $v$.

The *greedy dominating set algorithm* builds up a dominating set by placing the least numbered vertex that covers the most vertices in the dominating set. This procedure is repeated until all vertices are covered.

## Status

$\P$-complete (Stewart <a href="#ref-1" class="reference-citation">[1]</a>).

## Remarks

The reduction to show hardness is from [OHDVR]({{< relref "./a-2-10.md" >}}).

This greedy heuristic is used to find ``small'' dominating sets. It is well known that the general version of the problem called *Dominating Set* is $\NP$-complete (Garey and Johnson <a href="#ref-2" class="reference-citation">[2]</a>).
