## Given

Let $S$ be a finite set and $F$ be the free group generated by $S$. Let $\overline{S} = \\{s, s^{-1} \mid s \in S\\}$, where $s^{-1}$ denotes the inverse of $s$. Let $\overline{S}^\ast$ denote the set of all finite words over $\overline{S}$. Let $U = \\{u_1, \ldots, u_m\\}$, $V = \\{v_1, \ldots, v_p\\}$ $\subseteq \overline{S}^\ast$, where $m, p \in \N$.

## Problem

Is the group generated by $U$ a subgroup of the group generated by $V$?

## Status

$\P$-complete (Avenhaus and Madlener [[1]](#1)).

## Remarks

A variant of the Nielsen reduction algorithm can be used to show the problem is in $\P$. The reduction to show hardness is from [GWP]({{< relref "./a-8-6.md" >}}).

Since $\langle U \rangle$ is a subgroup of $\langle V \rangle$ if and only if $\langle U \cup V \rangle$ is normal in $\langle V \rangle$, it follows that the *Normal Subgroup Problem*, which is also in $\P$, is $\P$-complete. The problem of determining whether $\langle U \rangle$ is normal in $\langle U, x \rangle$ is also $\P$-complete [[1]](#1).
