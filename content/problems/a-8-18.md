## Given

A finitely presented algebra $A = (M, \mathcal{A}, \Gamma)$ and a pair of terms $x$ and $y$.

## Problem

Is $x \equiv y$?

## Definitions

Set $M$ is finite and consists of *symbols* and $\mathcal{A} : M \to \N$ defines the *arity* of each symbol. $M$ is partitioned into two sets: $G = \\{a \in M \mid \mathcal{A}(a) = 0\\}$ consists of *generator symbols* and $O = \\{a \in M \mid \mathcal{A}(a) > 0\\}$ consists of *operator symbols*. The set of *terms* over $M$ is the smallest subset of $M^\ast$ such that
1. all elements of $G$ are terms, and
2. if $\theta$ is $m$-ary and $x_1, \ldots, x_m$ are terms, then $\theta(x_1, \ldots, x_m)$ is a term.

Let $\tau$ denote the set of terms. Set $\Gamma$ consists of unordered pairs of terms called *axioms*. The congruence relation $\equiv$ is the smallest on $\tau$ satisfying the axioms of $\Gamma$.

## Status

$\P$-complete (Kozen <a href="#ref-1" class="reference-citation">[1]</a>).
