## Given

A finitely presented algebra $A = (M, \mathcal{A}, \Gamma)$ and a pair of terms $x$ and $y$. $M$ is a finite set of symbols and $\mathcal{A} : M \to \mathbb{N}$ defines the arity of each symbol. $M$ is partitioned into two sets: $G = \\{a \in M \mid \mathcal{A}(a) = 0\\}$ consists of generator symbols and $O = \\{a \in M \mid \mathcal{A}(a) > 0\\}$ consists of operator symbols. The set of terms over $M$ is the smallest subset of $M^*$ such that 1. all elements of $G$ are terms, and 2. if $\theta$ is $m$-ary and $x_1, \ldots, x_m$ are terms, then $\theta(x_1, \ldots, x_m)$ is a term. Let $\tau$ denote the set of terms. $\Gamma$ is a set of unordered pairs of terms called axioms. $\equiv$ is the smallest congruence relation on $\tau$ satisfying the axioms of $\Gamma$.

## Problem

Is $x \equiv y$?

## Status

$\P$-complete problem from Greenlaw et al. (1995), Problem A.8.18.
