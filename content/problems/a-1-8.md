## Given

An encoding $\alpha$ of an arithmetic circuit $\alpha$ with dyadic operations $+$, $-$, and $\ast$, together with inputs $x_1, \ldots, x_n$ from a ring.

## Problem

Does $\alpha$ on input $x_1, \ldots, x_n$ output 1?

## Status

P-complete problem from Greenlaw et al. (1995), Problem A.1.8.

## Remarks

The problem is not necessarily in $\mathsf{FP}$ for infinite rings like $\mathbb{Z}$ or $\mathbb{Q}$, since intermediate values need not be of polynomial length. It will be in $\mathsf{FP}$ for any finite ring, and remains $\mathsf{P}$-hard in any ring. It is also $\mathsf{P}$-hard to decide whether all gates in an arithmetic circuit over $\mathbb{Z}$ have "small" values, say values of a magnitude $2^{n^{O(1)}}$. Cucker and Torrecillas [73] look at related problems. The problem is in $\mathsf{NC}$ for circuits of degree $2^{(\log n)^{O(1)}}$, where the degree of a vertex is one for inputs, and $d_1+d_2$ (respectively, $\max(d_1, d_2)$) when the vertex is a product (respectively, sum) of the values computed by two vertices of degree $d_1$ and $d_2$ (Miller, Ramachandran, and Kaltofen [258], Valiant et al. [358]).
