## Given

An integer $n \times d$ matrix $A$ and an integer $n \times 1$ vector $b$.

## Problem

Is there a rational $d \times 1$ vector $x > 0$ such that $Ax \leq b$? (It is not required to find such an $x$.) By $x > 0$ we mean all components of $x$ are nonnegative and at least one is nonzero.

## Status

$\P$-complete (Khachian [[1]](#1), Valiant [[2]](#2)).

## Remarks

The reduction to show hardness is from [CVP]({{< relref "./a-1-1.md" >}}), and LI is in $\P$ by [[1]](#1).
Remains $\P$-complete if entries in $A$ and $b$ are restricted to $\\{0, 1\\}$. See also remarks for [LE]({{< relref "./a-4-2.md" >}}) and [LP]({{< relref "./a-4-3.md" >}}).
