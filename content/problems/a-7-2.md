## Given

A context-free grammar $G = (N, T, P, S)$.

## Problem

Is $L(G)$ empty?

## Status

$\P$-complete (Jones and Laaser [[1]](#1), Goldschlager [[2]](#2)).

## Remarks

Same reduction as for [CFGmem]({{< relref "./a-7-1.md" >}}) suffices, but it may also be reduced from [MCVP]({{< relref "./a-1-3.md" >}}) [[3]](#3).

Note, this reduction and the one for [CFGinf]({{< relref "./a-7-3.md" >}}), have no $\epsilon$-productions yet remain complete. The original proof of Jones and Laaser reduced [GEN]({{< relref "./a-6-7.md" >}}) to CFGempty. Their proof used the reduction for [CFGmem]({{< relref "./a-7-1.md" >}}), and instead checked if $L(G)$ is empty [[1]](#1).
