## Given

An undirected graph $G = (V, E)$ with positive integer weights on the edges.

## Problem

Find a matching of maximum weight. A matching is a subset of edges $E' \subseteq E$ such that no two edges in $E'$ share a common endpoint.

## Status

Open problem from Greenlaw et al. (1995), Problem B.1.3.

## Remarks

EWM is in $\mathsf{RNC}$ if all weights are polynomially bounded [1]. Even in this restricted case, the problem is not known to be in $\NC$. Define $\text{rank}(e)$ to be the position of edge $e$'s weight in the sorted list of edge weights, where the sorting is done in increasing order. Lexicographically First Maximal Matching, Problem B.8.2 (and hence all of $\mathsf{CC}$), is $\NC$ reducible to EWM, by assigning weight $2^{\text{rank}(e)}$ to edge $e$.
