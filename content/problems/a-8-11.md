## Given

Let $S$ be a finite set and $F$ be the free group generated by $S$. Let $\overline{S} = \\{s, s^{-1} \mid s \in S\\}$, where $s^{-1}$ denotes the inverse of $s$. Let $\overline{S}^\ast$ denote the set of all finite words over $\overline{S}$. Let $k \in \N$. Let $U = \\{u_1, \ldots, u_m\\} \subseteq \overline{S}^\ast$, where $m \in \N$.

## Problem

Does $\langle U \rangle$ have rank $k$? 

## Definitions

The *rank* of $\langle U \rangle$ is the number of elements in a minimal generating set.

## Status

$\P$-complete (Avenhaus and Madlener [[1]](#1)).

## Remarks

A variant of the Nielsen reduction algorithm can be used to show the problem is in $\P$. The reduction to show hardness is from [GI]({{< relref "./a-8-10.md" >}}).
