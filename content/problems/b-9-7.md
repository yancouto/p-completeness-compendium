## Given

An undirected graph $G = (V, E)$.

## Problem

Find a maximum matching of $G$. The concept of matching is defined in Problem B.1.3. A matching is maximum if no matching of larger cardinality exists.

## Status

Open (Ladner <a href="#ref-1" class="reference-citation">[1]</a>).

## Remarks

Feather shows that the problem of finding the size of a maximum matching is in $\mathsf{RNC}$ <a href="#ref-2" class="reference-citation">[2]</a>. Karp, Upfal, and Wigderson gave the first $\mathsf{RNC}$ algorithm for finding the maximum matching <a href="#ref-3" class="reference-citation">[3]</a>. A more efficient algorithm was given by Mulmuley, Vazirani, and Vazirani <a href="#ref-4" class="reference-citation">[4]</a>. Karloff shows how any $\mathsf{RNC}$ algorithm for matching can be made errorless <a href="#ref-5" class="reference-citation">[5]</a>. Maximum Edge-weighted Matching for unary edge weights and Maximum Vertex-weighted Matching for binary vertex weights are also known to be in $\mathsf{RNC}$ <a href="#ref-3" class="reference-citation">[3]</a><a href="#ref-4" class="reference-citation">[4]</a>.
