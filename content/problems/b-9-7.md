## Given

An undirected graph $G = (V, E)$.

## Problem

Find a maximum matching of $G$. The concept of matching is defined in Problem B.1.3. A matching is maximum if no matching of larger cardinality exists.

## Status

Open (Ladner [1]).

## Remarks

Feather shows that the problem of finding the size of a maximum matching is in $\mathsf{RNC}$ [2]. Karp, Upfal, and Wigderson gave the first $\mathsf{RNC}$ algorithm for finding the maximum matching [3]. A more efficient algorithm was given by Mulmuley, Vazirani, and Vazirani [4]. Karloff shows how any $\mathsf{RNC}$ algorithm for matching can be made errorless [5]. Maximum Edge-weighted Matching for unary edge weights and Maximum Vertex-weighted Matching for binary vertex weights are also known to be in $\mathsf{RNC}$ [3][4].
