## Given

An undirected graph $G = (V, E)$.

## Problem

Find a maximum matching of $G$. The concept of matching is defined in Problem B.1.3. A matching is maximum if no matching of larger cardinality exists.

## Status

Open problem from Greenlaw et al. (1995), Problem B.9.7.

## Remarks

Feather shows that the problem of finding the size of a maximum matching is in $\text{RNC}$ [1]. Karp, Upfal, and Wigderson gave the first $\text{RNC}$ algorithm for finding the maximum matching [2]. A more efficient algorithm was given by Mulmuley, Vazirani, and Vazirani [3]. Karloff shows how any $\text{RNC}$ algorithm for matching can be made errorless [4]. Maximum Edge-weighted Matching for unary edge weights and Maximum Vertex-weighted Matching for binary vertex weights are also known to be in $\text{RNC}$ [2][3].
