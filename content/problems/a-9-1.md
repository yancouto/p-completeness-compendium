## Given

An $n$-vertex polygon $Q$ that may contain holes, and a designated vertex $u$.

## Problem

Is there a vertical edge connecting to $u$ in the plane sweep triangulation of $Q$? The plane sweep triangulation is the triangulation produced by sweeping a horizontal line $L$ from top to bottom. When $L$ encounters a vertex $v$ of $Q$, each diagonal from $v$ to another vertex in $Q$, which does not cross a previously drawn diagonal, is added to the triangulation.

## Status

P-complete problem from Greenlaw et al. (1995), Problem A.9.1.

## Remarks

The problem of finding some arbitrary triangulation is in $\text{NC}$ (Goodrich [130]). If the polygon $Q$ is not allowed to have holes, the complexity of the problem is open. In [18] they conjecture that this restricted version is in $\text{NC}$.
