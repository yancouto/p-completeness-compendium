## Given

An encoding $\alpha$ of a Boolean circuit $\alpha$, inputs $x_1, \ldots, x_n$, and designated output $y$. Circuit $\alpha$ is constructed only of nand gates and is restricted to have fanout two for inputs and nand gates.

## Problem

Is output $y$ of $\alpha$ true on input $x_1, \ldots, x_n$?

## Status

P-complete problem from Greenlaw et al. (1995), Problem A.1.5.

## Remarks

Any complete basis of gates suffices, by the obvious simulation of nand gates in the other basis. For example, nor gates form a complete basis. NORCVP is defined analogously to NAND- CVP. See Post for a characterization of complete bases [292]. See the A.1. CIRCUIT COMPLEXITY 123 remarks for Problem A.1.1 for other bases, not necessarily complete, for which the associated Circuit Value Problem is still complete. Additional references on Boolean function bases and parallel complexity: [127], [281].
