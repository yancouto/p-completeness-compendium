## Given

An encoding $\overline{\alpha}$ of a Boolean circuit $\alpha$, inputs $x_1, \ldots, x_n$ and an output gate $y$. Circuit $\alpha$ is constructed only of NAND gates and is restricted to have fanout two for inputs and NAND gates.

## Problem

Is output $y$ of $\alpha$ true on inputs $x_1, \ldots, x_n$?

## Status

$\P$-complete under $\NC^1$ reductions (Greenlaw, Roover and Ruzzo [2, Theorem 6.2.4]).

## Remarks

Any complete basis of gates suffices, by the obvious simulation of NAND gates in the other basis. For example, NOR gates form a complete basis. NORCVP is defined analogously to NANDCVP. See Post for a characterization of complete bases [1]. See the remarks for [CVP]({{< relref "problems/a-1-1.md" >}}) for other bases, not necessarily complete, for which the associated Circuit Value Problem is still complete.
