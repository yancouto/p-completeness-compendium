## Given

A finite set $W$, a binary operation $\bullet$ on $W$ (presented as a table), a subset $V \subseteq W$, and $w \in W$.

## Problem

Is $w$ contained in the smallest subset of $W$ that contains $V$ and is closed under the operation $\bullet$?

## Status

$\P$-complete under $\NC^1$ reductions (Jones and Laaser <a href="#ref-3" class="reference-citation">[3]</a>, Barrington and McKenzie <a href="#ref-2" class="reference-citation">[2]</a>).

## Remarks

The reduction to show hardness is from [UNIT]({{< relref "./a-6-1.md" >}}) or [CVP]({{< relref "./a-1-1.md" >}}).
The problem remains $\P$-complete if $V$ is a singleton set and $\bullet$ is commutative (Barrington and McKenzie <a href="#ref-2" class="reference-citation">[2]</a>, <a href="#ref-3" class="reference-citation">[3]</a>). If $\bullet$ is associative, GEN is complete for $\mathsf{NLOG}$. The problem remains in $\P$ even with more than one operation.

The complexities of several other versions of GEN are addressed in <a href="#ref-2" class="reference-citation">[2]</a>. Under the appropriate definitions, it is known that approximating this problem is also $\P$-complete (Serna and Spirakis <a href="#ref-4" class="reference-citation">[4]</a>). Simons developed an $O(n)$ time parallel algorithm for the problem in 1987, where $n = |W|$ <a href="#ref-5" class="reference-citation">[5]</a>.
