## Given

An encoding $\overline{\alpha}$ of a planar Boolean circuit $\alpha$, inputs $x_1, \ldots, x_n$ and designated output $y$. 

## Problem

Is output $y$ of $\alpha$ true on inputs $x_1, \ldots, x_n$?

## Definitions

See [CVP]({{< relref "./a-1-1.md" >}}). A *planar* circuit is one whose graph can be drawn in the plane with no edges crossing.

## Status

$\P$-complete under $\NC^1$ reductions (Goldschlager [[1]](#1)).

## Remarks

The reduction is from [CVP]({{< relref "./a-1-1.md" >}}) and it's done via a planar cross-over circuit using planar XOR circuits. Monotone planar cross-over circuits do not exist, and in fact the monotone version of PCVP is in $\mathsf{LOGCFL} \subseteq \NC^2$ when all inputs appear on one face of the planar embedding (Dymond and Cook [[2]](#2)[[3]](#3), Goldschlager [[4]](#4), Mayr [[5]](#5)). The more general problem where inputs may appear anywhere is also in $\NC$ (Delcher and Kosaraju [[6]](#6), Kosaraju [[7]](#7)). A complete characterization of all bases from which it is possible to construct planar cross-over circuits is given in McColl [[8]](#8).
