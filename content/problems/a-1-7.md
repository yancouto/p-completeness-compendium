## Given

An encoding $\overline{\alpha}$ of a planar Boolean circuit $\alpha$, inputs $x_1, \ldots, x_n$ and designated output $y$. A planar circuit is one whose graph can be drawn in the plane with no edges crossing.

## Problem

Is output $y$ of $\alpha$ true on inputs $x_1, \ldots, x_n$?

## Status

$\P$-complete under $\NC^1$ reductions (Goldschlager <a href="#ref-1" class="reference-citation">[1]</a>).

## Remarks

The reduction is done via a planar cross-over circuit using planar XOR circuits. Monotone planar cross-over circuits do not exist, and in fact the monotone version of PCVP is in $\mathsf{LOGCFL} \subseteq \NC^2$ when all inputs appear on one face of the planar embedding (Dymond and Cook <a href="#ref-2" class="reference-citation">[2]</a><a href="#ref-3" class="reference-citation">[3]</a>, Goldschlager <a href="#ref-4" class="reference-citation">[4]</a>, Mayr <a href="#ref-5" class="reference-citation">[5]</a>). The more general problem where inputs may appear anywhere is also in $\NC$ (Delcher and Kosaraju <a href="#ref-6" class="reference-citation">[6]</a>, Kosaraju <a href="#ref-7" class="reference-citation">[7]</a>). A complete characterization of all bases from which it is possible to construct planar cross-over circuits is given in McColl <a href="#ref-8" class="reference-citation">[8]</a>.
