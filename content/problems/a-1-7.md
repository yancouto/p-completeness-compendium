## Given

An encoding $\alpha$ of a planar Boolean circuit $\alpha$, inputs $x_1, \ldots, x_n$, and designated output $y$. A planar circuit is one whose graph can be drawn in the plane with no edges crossing.

## Problem

Is output $y$ of $\alpha$ true on input $x_1, \ldots, x_n$?

## Status

P-complete problem from Greenlaw et al. (1995), Problem A.1.7.

## Remarks

It is easy to see that monotone planar cross-over networks do not exist, so the reduction above cannot be done in the monotone case. In fact, the monotone version of PCVP is in $\text{LOGCFL} \subseteq \text{NC}^2$ when all inputs appear on one face of the planar embedding (Dymond and Cook [99, 100], Goldschlager [124], Mayr [253]). The more general problem where inputs may appear anywhere is also in NC (Delcher and Kosaraju [87], Kosaraju [219]). A complete characterization of all bases from which it is possible to construct planar cross-over circuits is given in McColl [256].
