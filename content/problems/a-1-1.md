## Given

An encoding $\alpha$ of a Boolean circuit $\alpha$, inputs $x_1, \ldots, x_n$, and designated output $y$.

## Problem

Is output $y$ of $\alpha$ true on input $x_1, \ldots, x_n$?

## Status

P-complete problem from Greenlaw et al. (1995), Problem A.1.1.

## Remarks

For the two input basis of Boolean functions, it is known that CVP is P-complete except when the basis consists solely of or, consists solely of and, or consists of any or all of the following: xor, equivalence, and not (Goldschlager and Parberry [127], Parberry [281]).
