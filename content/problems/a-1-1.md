## Given

A Boolean circuit $\alpha$, inputs $x_1, \ldots, x_n$ and an output gate $y$.

## Problem

Is the output $y$ of $\alpha$ true on inputs $x_1, \ldots, x_n$?

## Status

$\P$-complete under $\NC^1$ reductions [2, Theorem 4.2.2].

## Remarks

The CVP is the most fundamental $\P$-complete problem, in fact, its one of the few where $\P$-completeness is proved directly instead of through reductions from other $\P$-complete problems.

A Boolean circuit $\alpha$ is a labeled directed acyclic graph. Each vertex (gate) is labeled INPUT, AND, OR, or NOT, and computes its corresponding function over its $n$ inputs, whose values are given as $x_1, \ldots, x_n \in \{0,1\}$.

For the two input basis of Boolean functions, it is known that CVP is $P$-complete except when the basis consists solely of or, consists solely of and, or consists of any or all of the following: XOR, EQUIVALENCE, and NOT (Goldschlager and Parberry [1]).
