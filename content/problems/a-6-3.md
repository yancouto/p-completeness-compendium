## Given

A set $S$ of Horn clauses in the propositional calculus.

## Problem

Is $S$ satisfiable?

## Status

$\P$-complete (Kasif <a href="#ref-1" class="reference-citation">[1]</a>, Plaisted <a href="#ref-2" class="reference-citation">[2]</a>).

## Remarks

The reduction to show hardness is from [AGAP]({{< relref "./a-2-3.md" >}}).
The problem remains $\P$-complete when there are at most three literals per clause <a href="#ref-2" class="reference-citation">[2]</a>. Plaisted has shown that two problems involving proofs of restricted depth are also $\P$-complete. They are the *Two Literal Horn Clause Unique Matching Problem* and the *Three Literal Horn Clause Problem* <a href="#ref-2" class="reference-citation">[2]</a>. Kasif observes that PHCS remains $\P$-complete when the set of clauses is restricted so that implications have at most two atoms on their right-hand side <a href="#ref-3" class="reference-citation">[3]</a>.
