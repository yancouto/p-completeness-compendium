## Given

A set $S$ of Horn clauses in the propositional calculus.

## Problem

Is $S$ satisfiable?

## Status

$\P$-complete (Kasif [1], Plaisted [2]).

## Remarks

The problem remains $\P$-complete when there are at most three literals per clause [2]. Plaisted has shown that two problems involving proofs of restricted depth are also $\P$-complete. They are the Two Literal Horn Clause Unique Matching Problem and the Three Literal Horn Clause Problem [2]. Kasif observes that PHCS remains $\P$-complete when the set of clauses is restricted so that implications have at most two atoms on their right-hand side Kasif [3].
