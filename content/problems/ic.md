## Input

Let $S$ be a finite set and $F$ be the free group generated by $S$. Let $\overline{S} = \\{s, s^{-1} \mid s \in S\\}$, where $s^{-1}$ denotes the inverse of $s$. Let $\overline{S}^\ast$ denote the set of all finite words over $\overline{S}$. Let $x, y \in \overline{S}^\ast$. Let $U = \\{u_1, \ldots, u_m\\}$, $V = \\{v_1, \ldots, v_p\\} \subseteq \overline{S}^\ast$, where $m = p \in \N$.

## Problem

Is $\langle U \rangle x \cap y \langle V \rangle$ nonempty?

## Status

$\P$-complete (Avenhaus and Madlener [[1]](#1)).

## Remarks

A polynomial time algorithm for the problem is given in [[1]](#1). The reduction to show hardness is from an arbitrary polynomial time Turing machine.

The *Intersection of Right Cosets Problem* and the *Intersection of Left Cosets Problem* are subproblems of the Intersection of Cosets Problems and they are both $\P$-complete as well. For example, the Right Coset Problem is $\P$-complete since $\langle U \rangle x \cap \langle V \rangle y$ is nonempty if and only if $\langle U \rangle xy^{-1} \cap e \langle V \rangle$ is nonempty, where $e$ denotes the empty word.
