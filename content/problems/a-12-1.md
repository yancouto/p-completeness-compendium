## Given

A string $x$, a description $M$ of a Turing machine $M$, and an integer $t$ coded in unary. (To be precise, the input is the string $x\\#\overline{M}\\#^t$, where $\\#$ is a delimiter character not otherwise present in the string.)

## Problem

Does $M$ accept $x$ within $t$ steps?

## Status

$\P$-complete (Greenlaw, Roover and Ruzzo [3, Theorem 4.1.2]).

## Remarks

Buss and Goldsmith show a variant of this problem is complete with respect to *quasilinear time reductions* for $\mathsf{N^mP_l}$, a subclass of $\P$. See [2] for the appropriate definitions. They also show variants of [TopCVP]({{< relref "./a-1-2.md" >}}) and [CFGempty]({{< relref "./a-7-2.md" >}}) are complete in this setting.
