## Given

Two $n$-bit positive integers $a$ and $b$.

## Problem

Compute the greatest common divisor (gcd) of $a$ and $b$, denoted $\gcd(a, b)$.

## Status

Open problem from Greenlaw et al. (1995), Problem B.5.3.

## Remarks

For $n$th degree polynomials $p, q \in \mathbb{Q}[x]$, computing $\gcd(p, q)$ is in $\text{NC}^2$ via an $\text{NC}^1$ reduction to Determinant (Cook and Sethi [69], Borodin, Cook, and Pippenger [42]). IntegerGCD is $\text{NC}^1$ reducible to Short Vectors Dimension 2, Problem B.7.2 [368]. Kannan, Miller, and Rudolph give a sublinear CRCW-PRAM algorithm for computing the gcd of two $n$-bit integers [184]. Their algorithm requires $O(n \log \log n / \log n)$ time and $n^2 (\log n)^2$ processors. Chor and Goldreich give a CRCW-PRAM algorithm for the same problem with time bound $O(n / \log n)$ and processor bound $n^{1+\epsilon}$, for any positive constant $\epsilon$ [56]. Sorenson presents an algorithm called the $k$-ary gcd algorithm [335, 336] whose sequential version is practical and whose parallel version has bounds that match those given in [56]. Also see (Deng [90], Lin-Kriz and Pan [237], Shallcross, Pan, and Lin-Kriz [329]) for additional research on IntegerGCD and related problems.
