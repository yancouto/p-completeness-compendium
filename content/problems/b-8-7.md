## Given

A telephone line with a fixed channel capacity $k$, an integer $l$, and a sequence of calls $(s_1, f_1), \ldots, (s_n, f_n)$. 

## Problem

Is the $l^\text{th}$ call serviced?

## Definitions

Here, $s_i$ ($f_i$) denotes the starting (respectively, finishing) time of the $i^\text{th}$ call. 
The $i^\text{th}$ call can be serviced at time $s_i$ if the number of calls being served at that time is less than $k$. If the call cannot be served, it is discarded. When a call is completed, a channel is freed up.

## Status

Open (Ramachandran and Wang [[1]](#1)).

## Remarks

This problem is in $\CC$, that is, it is equivalent to [CCVP]({{< relref "./b-8-1.md" >}}).
Ramachandran and Wang give an $O(\min(\sqrt{n}, k) \log n)$ time EREW-PRAM algorithm that uses $n$ processors for solving TC [[1]](#1). Also see [NetSim]({{< relref "./a-12-8.md" >}}).
