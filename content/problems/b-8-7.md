## Given

A telephone line with a fixed channel capacity $k$, an integer $l$, and a sequence of calls $(s_1, f_1), \ldots, (s_n, f_n)$, where $s_i$ ($f_i$) denotes the starting (respectively, finishing) time of the $i$th call. The $i$th call can be serviced at time $s_i$ if the number of calls being served at that time is less than $k$. If the call cannot be served, it is discarded. When a call is completed, a channel is freed up.

## Problem

Is the $l$th call serviced?

## Status

Open problem from Greenlaw et al. (1995), Problem B.8.7.

## Remarks

TC is $\NC$ equivalent to Comparator Circuit Value, Problem B.8.1 [1]. Ramachandran and Wang give an $O(\min(\sqrt{n}, k) \log n)$ time EREW-PRAM algorithm that uses $n$ processors for solving TC [1]. Also see Problem A.12.8.

The problems in appendix B.9 are all known to be in $\mathsf{RNC}$ or $\mathsf{FRNC}$, but not known to be in $\NC$ or $\mathsf{FNC}$. A proof that any of them is $\P$-complete would be almost as unexpected as a proof that $\NC$ equals $\P$. Of course, $\NC \subseteq \mathsf{RNC}$ but $\mathsf{RNC}$ and $\P$ are at present incomparable. That is, the classes cannot be related using the $\subseteq$ relation. See Section 3.2.3 for more discussion of these issues. Here we use $\mathsf{RNC}$ ($\NC$) to represent both $\mathsf{RNC}$ and $\mathsf{FRNC}$ (respectively, $\NC$ and $\mathsf{FNC}$) depending on context.
