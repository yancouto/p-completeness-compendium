## Given

An undirected graph $G = (V, E)$ with a numbering on the vertices in $V$, and two designated vertices $u$ and $v$.

## Problem

Is there an elimination order on $V$, $v_1, \ldots, v_n$, satisfying the properties that $u$ is eliminated before $v$ and for $1 \leq i \leq n$, $v_i$ is the lowest numbered vertex of maximum degree in the $(i-1)$-st remaining subgraph of $G$? An elimination order is a sequence of vertices ordered as they and their corresponding edges are to be deleted from the graph.

## Status

P-complete problem from Greenlaw et al. (1995), Problem A.2.10.

## Remarks

The problem addresses the existence of a specific vertex elimination order with structural constraints related to vertex numbering and degree properties in graph subgraphs.
