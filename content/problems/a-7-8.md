## Given

A two-way deterministic pushdown automaton $M = (Q, \Sigma, \Gamma, \delta, q_0, Z_0, F)$ and a string $x$.

## Problem

Is $x$ accepted by $M$?

## Status

$\P$-complete (Cook <a href="#ref-4" class="reference-citation">[4]</a>, Galil <a href="#ref-1" class="reference-citation">[1]</a><a href="#ref-2" class="reference-citation">[2]</a>, Ladner <a href="#ref-3" class="reference-citation">[3]</a>).

## Remarks

See, for example, Hopcroft and Ullman <a href="#ref-5" class="reference-citation">[5]</a> for a definition of 2DPDAs. Cook gives a direct simulation of a polynomial time Turing machine by a logarithmic space *auxiliary* pushdown automaton <a href="#ref-4" class="reference-citation">[4]</a>. Galil shows the existence of a $\P$-complete language accepted by a 2DPDA, in effect showing that the logarithmic space work tape is not crucial to Cook's simulation <a href="#ref-1" class="reference-citation">[1]</a><a href="#ref-2" class="reference-citation">[2]</a>. (See also Sudborough <a href="#ref-6" class="reference-citation">[6]</a> for a general reduction of auxiliary PDAs to ordinary PDAs.) Ladner gives a much more direct proof by observing that a suitably encoded version of [CVP]({{< relref "./a-1-1.md" >}}) is solvable by a 2DPDA, basically by doing a depth-first search of the circuit, using the stack for backtracking <a href="#ref-3" class="reference-citation">[3]</a>.

Remains in $\P$ when generalized to nondeterministic and/or logarithmic space auxiliary PDAs <a href="#ref-4" class="reference-citation">[4]</a>. When restricted to one-way PDAs, or other polynomial time PDAs, even with a logarithmic space work tape, the problem is in $\NC$; specifically, it is complete for $\mathsf{LOGDCFL}$ in the deterministic case, and for $\mathsf{LOGCFL} = \mathsf{SAC}^1$ in the nondeterministic case.
