## Given

A two-way deterministic pushdown automaton $M = (Q, \Sigma, \Gamma, \delta, q_0, Z_0, F)$ and a string $x$.

## Problem

Is $x$ accepted by $M$?

## Status

$\P$-complete (Cook [4], Galil [1][2], Ladner [3]).

## Remarks

Remains in $\P$ when generalized to nondeterministic and/or logarithmic space auxiliary PDAs. When restricted to one-way PDAs, or other polynomial time PDAs, even with a logarithmic space work tape, the problem is in $\NC$; specifically, it is complete for $\mathsf{LOGDCFL}$ in the deterministic case, and for $\mathsf{LOGCFL} = \mathsf{SAC}^1$ in the nondeterministic case.
