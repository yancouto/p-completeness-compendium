## Given

An encoding $\overline{\alpha}$ of a Boolean circuit constructed of AND, OR, and NOT gates, plus inputs $x = x_1, \ldots, x_n$. The circuit's $m$ output values $y = y_1, \ldots, y_m$.

## Problem

Is the circuit's output a local maximum among the neighbors of $x$ when $y$ is viewed as a binary number? The *neighbors* of $x$ are Boolean vectors of length $n$ whose Hamming distance differs from $x$ by one. That is, they can be obtained from $x$ by flipping one bit.

## Status

$\P$-complete (Johnson, Papadimitriou, and Yannakakis [[1]](#1)).

## Remarks

The reduction to show hardness is from [MCVP]({{< relref "./a-1-3.md" >}}).
The complementary problem, called the *FLIP Verification Problem*, in which the output is minimized, is also $\P$-complete [[1]](#1). The general problems MAXFLIP and FLIP are $\mathsf{PLS}$-complete [[1]](#1). [$\mathsf{PLS}$](https://complexityzoo.net/Complexity_Zoo:P#pls) stands for *polynomially local search*. We note for most problems that are $\mathsf{PLS}$-complete the problem of verifying local optimality turned out to be $\P$-complete. However, Krentel shows that this is not always the case [[2]](#2).
